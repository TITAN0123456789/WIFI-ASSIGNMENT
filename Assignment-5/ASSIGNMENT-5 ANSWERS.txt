1. Key Features of Wi-Fi 6, 6E and 7
Wi-Fi 5 (802.11ac)
Operates in 5 GHz only
Uses OFDM
Supports MU-MIMO (downlink only)
Max speed ≈ 3.5 Gbps
Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax)
Operates in 2.4 GHz & 5 GHz
Introduces OFDMA
Supports UL + DL MU-MIMO
Adds Target Wake Time (TWT)
Max speed ≈ 9.6 Gbps
Wi-Fi 6E
Extends Wi-Fi 6 to 6 GHz band
More channels → less congestion
Lower latency
Wi-Fi 7 (802.11be)
Supports 320 MHz channels
Uses 4096-QAM
Introduces Multi-Link Operation (MLO)
Max speed ≈ 46 Gbps
Comparison
Feature	Wi-Fi 5	Wi-Fi 6	Wi-Fi 6E	Wi-Fi 7
Band	5 GHz	2.4/5 GHz	+6 GHz	All bands
Efficiency	Medium	High	Very High	Extremely High
Latency	Medium	Low	Lower	Ultra-low

2. Role of OFDMA in Wi-Fi 6

OFDMA (Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiple Access):

Divides channel into subcarriers (RU – Resource Units)
Allows multiple users simultaneously
Example:
Channel → Split into 4 RUs
User1 → RU1
User2 → RU2
User3 → RU3
User4 → RU4
Benefits:
Reduced latency
Better spectrum utilization
Efficient for dense environments

3. Target Wake Time (TWT)

TWT allows devices to schedule wake-up times.

Working:
Device sleeps → wakes at scheduled time → transmits → sleeps again
Benefits for IoT:
Saves battery life
Reduces network congestion
Improves efficiency

4. Significance of 6 GHz Band (Wi-Fi 6E)
Provides more spectrum (1200 MHz approx.)
Supports more non-overlapping channels
Less interference (no legacy devices)
Advantages:
Higher throughput
Lower latency
Cleaner spectrum

5. Wi-Fi 6 vs Wi-Fi 6E
Feature	Wi-Fi 6	Wi-Fi 6E
Band	2.4/5 GHz	+6 GHz
Range	Higher	Slightly lower
Interference	Moderate	Very low
Bandwidth	Limited	High

6. Innovations in Wi-Fi 7

Major features:

320 MHz channel bandwidth
4096-QAM modulation
Multi-Link Operation (MLO)
Improved MU-MIMO
Lower latency (<1 ms)

7. Multi-Link Operation (MLO)

MLO allows a device to use multiple bands simultaneously.

Example:
Device → 2.4 GHz + 5 GHz + 6 GHz simultaneously
Impact:
Higher throughput
Reduced latency
Better reliability

8. 802.11k and 802.11v
802.11k (Radio Resource Management)
Provides neighbor reports
Helps client choose best AP
802.11v (Network Management)
AP suggests better AP to client
Load balancing

9. Fast BSS Transition (802.11r)

802.11r enables fast roaming between APs.

Working:
Pre-authentication with target AP
Reduces handoff delay
Benefit:
Seamless movement
Useful in VoIP, video calls

10. Combined Roaming (802.11k + v + r)
Working Together:
802.11k
Provides AP list
802.11v
Suggests best AP
802.11r
Performs fast authentication
Result:
Client moves → detects AP → switches instantly → no packet loss
